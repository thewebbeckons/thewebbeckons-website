# From Mockup to Market

Taking a design from concept to production is a journey that requires careful planning, clear communication, and a solid development process.

## The Discovery Phase

Before opening Figma, it's crucial to understand the problem you're solving. This means:

- Talking to stakeholders about their goals
- Understanding user needs through research
- Defining success metrics upfront

## Design Iteration

Design is rarely linear. I typically go through several rounds of:

1. **Low-fidelity wireframes** - Quick exploration of layouts
2. **High-fidelity mockups** - Detailed visual design
3. **Interactive prototypes** - Testing flows and interactions

## Development Handoff

A smooth handoff requires:

- Clear component specifications
- Documented design tokens
- Accessible design assets

## Launch and Beyond

The work doesn't stop at launch. Monitoring user behavior, gathering feedback, and iterating based on real data is essential for long-term success.

> "The best designs are never finished—they evolve with user needs."

## Key Takeaways

- Invest time in discovery before jumping to solutions
- Prototype early and test often
- Document everything for smoother handoffs
- Plan for iteration post-launch
